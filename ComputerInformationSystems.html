Schools That Offer Computer Information Systems

Mount Royal’s Bachelor of Computer Information Systems (BCIS) stands out for its balance between technical computing and business skills. Small class sizes create a supportive learning environment where students can work closely with professors and peers. The program emphasizes hands-on projects, giving you the chance to build real-world systems before graduation. Calgary is a growing tech hub, so students gain access to internships, networking, and job opportunities right in the city. MRU also offers strong co-op and work experience programs to bridge classroom knowledge with workplace practice. Students learn not only programming and systems analysis, but also project management and organizational communication. The degree is designed to prepare graduates for both IT management and development roles, giving flexibility in career paths. Employers value the program’s blend of technical depth and soft skills. Overall, Mount Royal’s BCIS is ideal if you want a practical, career-focused degree with a strong business-IT connection.

UFV’s Bachelor of Computer Information Systems is one of Canada’s most established CIS degrees, with multiple specialization options. Students can focus on Software Development, Systems and Networking, or Security, tailoring the program to career interests. The curriculum blends computing with communication, math, and business, ensuring a well-rounded skillset. UFV offers co-op education, meaning students graduate with work experience and industry connections. The program also has pathways to study abroad, broadening international exposure. Located in British Columbia, UFV connects students with opportunities in Vancouver’s expanding tech industry. Class sizes are mid-range, giving a balance of resources and personal attention. Employers recognize UFV grads as versatile professionals who can move into both technical and managerial roles. The program is especially strong if you want the ability to specialize while still keeping career options open.

Okanagan College’s BCIS degree is designed to merge computing, mathematics, business, and communications into one comprehensive program. The four-year curriculum ensures students are not just coders but also problem-solvers who understand business needs. Being in Kelowna, a region with a growing tech sector and startups, students benefit from local partnerships and internships. The program is very applied in nature, with practical labs and team-based projects that mimic real IT workplace environments. Graduates leave prepared for a range of roles, from software development to systems analysis and project management. The program also emphasizes communication skills and teamwork, which are critical in IT. Okanagan College is known for a supportive campus community, smaller classes, and approachable instructors. Its CIS program is well suited for those who want a broad IT education that combines technical and business perspectives.

Athabasca’s Bachelor of Science in Computing and Information Systems is fully online and open, making it one of the most flexible CIS programs in Canada. Students can start at almost any time of year, study at their own pace, and balance learning with work or personal commitments. This flexibility is especially attractive for working professionals or those outside major cities. The program combines computer science fundamentals with information systems management, covering topics from programming to databases and networking. Students can also choose minors, such as Business Administration, AI, or Information Systems Management, to customize their degree. Athabasca’s online platform emphasizes independent learning but also provides online forums, tutor support, and resources. Graduates develop both technical and managerial skills, preparing them for careers in IT, consulting, and management. Employers value Athabasca’s CIS degree because it shows both discipline and adaptability. Overall, it’s a strong option if you want a recognized CIS degree without being tied to a campus location.

Windsor’s BSc Honours in Computer Information Systems offers the benefits of a research-driven university combined with co-op opportunities. Students gain practical work experience during their degree, which strengthens job prospects upon graduation. The program is structured to provide a strong foundation in computer science while emphasizing information systems, databases, and organizational IT needs. Windsor’s location right across the border from Detroit offers unique cross-border job and networking opportunities in both Canada and the U.S. Class sizes in computing are moderate, providing access to resources while still allowing student-professor interaction. The program integrates business and management concepts, preparing students for IT leadership roles as well as technical development. Windsor also has strong industry partnerships, leading to good graduate employment outcomes. The program appeals to students who want a comprehensive CIS education with strong career readiness through co-op.
