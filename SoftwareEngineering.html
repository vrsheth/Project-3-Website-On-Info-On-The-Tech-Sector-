Schools That Offer Software Engineering

The University of Calgary’s (U of C) Software Engineering program is strong because it combines a solid foundation in computer science with practical engineering principles. Students learn not only how to code, but also how to design, build, and manage complex software systems in a structured, team-based environment. The program is accredited by the Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board (CEAB), which means graduates can pursue their Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) designation. With access to co-op opportunities, research projects, and connections to Calgary’s growing tech sector, students gain valuable hands-on experience and industry exposure. Overall, it prepares graduates with both the technical depth and professional skills needed for diverse software careers.

Class Reuqirements (Compared To Alberta High Schools)
  • English Langauge Arts 30-1
  • Math 30-1
  • Math 31
  • Chemistry 30
  • Physics 30 Or Biology 30
Competitive Average: High 80s
*Note: University Of Calgary does require all engineering students to do a common first year of engineering.*
The University of Alberta’s Software Engineering program is well-regarded for its strong mix of computer science and engineering. It’s accredited by the CEAB, so graduates can work toward their P.Eng. designation. Students benefit from cutting-edge research opportunities, industry connections in Edmonton’s growing tech sector, and access to co-op placements that provide real-world experience. The program emphasizes teamwork, problem-solving, and innovation, preparing graduates for careers in software development, systems design, and emerging technologies.

The University of Waterloo’s Software Engineering program is one of the most recognized in Canada. Jointly offered by the Faculty of Mathematics and the Faculty of Engineering, it blends deep computer science knowledge with engineering principles. The program is CEAB-accredited, allowing graduates to pursue a P.Eng. designation. Waterloo is especially known for its world-class co-op program, giving students up to two years of paid industry experience with leading tech companies in Canada and abroad. With strong ties to global tech hubs and a reputation for innovation, it provides an excellent launchpad for careers in software development, AI, and entrepreneurship.

The University of Ottawa’s Software Engineering program combines a strong foundation in computer science with engineering design and problem-solving. It is CEAB-accredited, giving graduates the pathway to a P.Eng. designation. The program offers bilingual study options (English and French), co-op placements, and opportunities to work on real-world projects with Ottawa’s thriving tech sector, including companies in software, AI, and cybersecurity. Students graduate with both technical expertise and professional skills that prepare them for diverse careers in technology.
