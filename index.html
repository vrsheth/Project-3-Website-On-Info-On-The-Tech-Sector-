Why Choose The Tech Sector

The tech sector is one of the fastest-growing industries worldwide, offering students a future-proof and impactful career path. With the rise of artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and data analytics, the demand for skilled tech professionals continues to surge. The World Economic Forum estimates that AI alone will create 97 million new jobs by 2025, while global spending on cloud services is projected to exceed $1 trillion by 2027.

These advancements are transforming every sector — from healthcare to finance — and students with coding, analytical, or system design skills are uniquely positioned to drive this change. Tech careers not only offer competitive salaries and remote flexibility but also provide the opportunity to solve real-world problems and innovate on a global scale. Choosing the tech field means building a career that is resilient, high-impact, and central to shaping the future.

A Brief History of Computers

Early Mechanical Computers (1822–1930):

Charles Babbage's Difference Engine laid the foundation for computing, introducing key modern ideas.

Electro-Mechanical Computers (1930s):

Machines like the Differential Analyzer used motors and gears to automate math operations.

Electronic Computers (1940s):

Colossus was one of the first electronic computers used to decrypt German codes during WWII.

The First Commercial Computers (1950s):

UNIVAC I brought computing to government and business operations.

Transistor Computers (Mid-1950s):

Transistors allowed faster and smaller computers than ever before.

The Microchip and Microprocessor (1960s):

Silicon chips made computing even more affordable and accessible.

Personal Computers (1970s):

Hobbyist kits like the Altair 8800 helped launch Apple and Microsoft.

The Early Notebooks and Laptops (1980s–1990s):

Laptops revolutionized mobile work, from the Osborne 1 to the ThinkPad.
